# Snap

> **Moved.** Snap is no longer an inspector section. The toggles
> retired from the inspector in s150 and now live in a toolbar
> popover.

## Where to find Snap now

The **Snap** controls live in a popover anchored to the Snap
button in the toolbar.

- **Master toggle:** press **Q**, or click the Snap button in
  the toolbar. The button shows on/off state visually.
- **Per-kind toggles:** **right-click** the Snap button to open
  the popover. It lists the same six toggles that used to live
  in this section — **Snap to guides**, **Snap to grid**,
  **Snap to margins**, **Snap to nodes**, **Snap to edges**,
  **Snap to centers**.

The settings still round-trip with the document and synchronise
with the project, exactly as they did when the section was here.

## Why this moved

Snap is a workflow setting more than a document setting — you
adjust it as you work, often without changing the rest of the
inspector context. Putting the toggles next to the tool that
acts on them (and a single key, Q, away) keeps them close at
hand without you having to scroll the inspector to reach them.

## Where to next

- **Q** is the master snap toggle — see **Keyboard shortcuts**
  (11.2) for the complete tool-key reference.
- The four geometry sources Snap can catch on still have their
  own inspector documentation: **Guides** (5.3.3) in the Object
  group, **Grid** (5.3.4) and **Margins** (5.3.5) inside the
  Theme disclosure (5.3.8).
