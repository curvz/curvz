# Welcome to Curvz

Curvz is a vector editor for Linux, focused on icons.

This is the manual. It is a single window inside the app, organised
into chapters that mirror the editor's surface. Every panel, every
tool, every dialog you can reach in Curvz has a page here, and the
sidebar on the left navigates the lot of them. The chapter and section
numbers on filenames are administrative — what you see in the sidebar
are clean topic titles.

If you are new to Curvz, the next two pages are the suggested order:

- **About Curvz** (1.2) — what Curvz is for, and what it deliberately
  isn't. Worth a minute before you commit serious work.
- **Workspace tour** (1.3) — a labelled walk around the window, naming
  every region the rest of the manual will refer back to.

After that, the manual fans out by topic and you can pick what is
relevant to the work in front of you. The **Reference** chapter (11)
holds the keyboard-shortcut cheatsheet and a troubleshooting page;
both are worth a bookmark.

The manual is read inside the app. There is no online copy and no
external dependency — every page, every screenshot, ships in the same
binary as the editor. Pressing **F1** anywhere in Curvz opens this
window.
