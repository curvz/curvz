# Welcome to Curvz

### ![Scott — the designer of Curvz](img/scott-bug.svg) Hi — I'm Scott, the designer of Curvz. Welcome.

## Why Curvz?

I wanted a precise, easy-to-use, lightweight tool to do most of my design
work. Developers need icons. Font designers need glyphs. CNC technicians
need real plot points. Curvz can do all of that, and it can do a lot more
besides — if you ever find yourself thinking *"I wish Curvz did…"*, it
probably does. You may be pleasantly surprised at how versatile it can be.

It is not an end-all be-all product. It is not a new Illustrator or Inkscape. It
is Curvz, and that means something that fills a niche. I expect you will
find uses for it I never thought of, and ways around design problems no
one has hit before. Curvz is nimble. I don't think there is another tool
quite like it. And that is why I made it — nothing else did what I wanted.

If you read one page in this manual before you go anywhere else, read
**1.4 How Curvz thinks about size**. It is the load-bearing idea the rest
of the tool quietly assumes, and it is the difference between Curvz feeling
natural and Curvz feeling like it is fighting you. A few minutes there will
save you an afternoon later.

After that, two more pages are worth a few minutes each:

- **About Curvz** (1.2) — what Curvz is for, and what it deliberately isn't.
- **Workspace tour** (1.3) — a labelled walk around the window, naming
  every region the rest of the manual will refer back to.

The remaining chapters fan out by topic; pick what is relevant to the work
in front of you. **Chapter 11 (Reference)** holds the macros page, the

## A note on the welcome animation

If you just watched Curvz draw something into a fresh tab on launch, that
was the welcome animation playing one of the SVGs from
`~/.config/curvz/welcome/`. Drop your own SVGs in there and Curvz will
pick one at random next time it launches. Empty folder, and you get the
bundled `scott-bug.svg` instead. Press **Esc** during playback to skip
it. You can turn the whole thing off in the inspector under
**Application ▸ Startup**.

That animation is not a special-purpose feature — it is Curvz running
a tiny script, the same kind any user can write. If you want to replay
your favourite SVG on demand (or build a longer ceremony than one
file), the **Animating SVGs** page under Addendums ▸ Developer shows
the composition pattern.

## A note on the manual itself

The manual lives inside the app. There is no online copy and no external
dependency — every page, every screenshot, ships in the same binary as
the editor. Pressing **F1** anywhere in Curvz opens this window. The
sidebar is a nested outline — chapters expand to show their sections,
each row labelled with its section number and topic title.
